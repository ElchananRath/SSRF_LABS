Takeaway

When the application does not reflect the full response—or omits useful error detail—direct confirmation of SSRF can be hard. Out-of-band (OOB) interaction is a standard answer: coerce the vulnerable server to issue a DNS query or HTTP(S) request to infrastructure you control, then correlate the hit with your exploit attempt.

OOB techniques are widely used for blind SSRF, SSRF-to-internal pivoting, and mapping reachability where inline output is unavailable or filtered.
