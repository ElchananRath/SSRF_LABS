Takeaway

URL and scheme validation is easy to get wrong. Naïve substring checks, case folding, decoding order, and parser inconsistencies often let a payload slip through after a minor transformation—exactly what attackers automate.

Defense in depth means parsing with a single canonical model, validating against an explicit allowlist of hosts/schemes/ports, and refusing to “helpfully” rewrite user input in ways that undo your own checks.
