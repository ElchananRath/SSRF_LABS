Takeaway

SSRF is not always the end state. When fetched content is passed into a template engine, log renderer, PDF generator, or other “string becomes code” layer, you can chain into Server-Side Template Injection (SSTI) and often achieve remote code execution.

The combined pattern—untrusted fetch plus unsafe render—is a severity multiplier: the same SSRF that looked like “read-only” cross-origin access becomes a path to execute logic on the server.
